Preventing stroke

Up to 80% of strokes are preventable. Small, consistent lifestyle changes make a significant difference.

🥗 Healthy diet

Eat plenty of fresh fruits and vegetables. Choose foods low in saturated fats, trans fat, and cholesterol. Limit salt to lower blood pressure — a major stroke risk factor. High cholesterol and high blood pressure significantly increase your risk.

🏃 Regular exercise

Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate activity most days. Exercise helps reduce hypertension, diabetes, high cholesterol, and stress — all key stroke risk factors. Even a daily walk makes a meaningful difference.

💉 Control blood pressure

High blood pressure is the single biggest modifiable stroke risk factor. Monitor it regularly, reduce salt intake, eat more fruits and vegetables, and take prescribed medication consistently. Target below 120/80 mmHg.

🚭 Stop smoking

Smoking narrows arteries and makes blood more likely to clot, significantly raising stroke risk. Talk to your doctor about cessation support, medications, or nicotine replacement. The benefits begin within hours of quitting.

🩸 Manage diabetes

Keep blood sugar levels within target range through medication, balanced diet, and exercise. Regular monitoring, following your doctor's advice, and maintaining a healthy weight are all critical for people with diabetes.

🧘 Manage stress

Chronic stress raises blood pressure and can lead to unhealthy coping habits. Try breathing exercises, regular physical activity, and addressing root causes. Seek professional support for persistent anxiety or depression.
