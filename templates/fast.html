F.A.S.T

F.A.S.T is an easy way to detect some immediate signs and symptoms of stroke and prompt immediate actions:

Face drooping

Does one side of the face droop? You can ask the person to smile and confirm if their face is symmetrical

Arm weakness

Is one of the arm numb or weak? You can ask the person to raise both arms and check if one of it drifts downwards

Speech difficulty

Is the person not able to speak or understand? You can ask the person to repeat a simple sentence

Time To Call

Time to call for help from the nearest hospital if any of these symptoms are observed in an individual

Although two third of cases of Stroke condition occur abruptly and without people noticing, about one third of the cases do have warning signs. Some symptoms occur at the onset of stroke and an ambulance should be called immediately. They include: Loss of strength in one part of the body, One-sided numbness, Partial loss of vision on one side, Loss of balance, Swallowing difficulties, and acute confusion. These signs may last for a few seconds.
